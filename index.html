SPATIAL
INTELLIGENCE.

The strategic contribution of AI and geomatics to territorial decision-making in Algeria.

2026/06/21 at USTHB, Algiers, Algeria.

01 / Vision

Transforming georeferenced data into strategic clarity.

In a context of profound spatial mutations, artificial intelligence (AI) and geomatics are establishing themselves as major strategic levers.

"The challenge is to transform this data into relevant information, directly accessible to territorial stakeholders."

The ist26 seminar creates an interdisciplinary space bringing together researchers, professionals, and institutions to build smart, adaptive, and sustainable territories.

The Pillars.

Four strategic pillars for research and development.

01

Rural Dynamics

Revitalizing territories, identifying assets, and modeling agricultural and land dynamics using AI to counter the loss of attractiveness.

02

Smart Cities

Optimizing network organization, planning land use, and steering policies for mobility and inclusive housing.

03

Climate & Forecasting

Cross-referencing climate and environmental data to model risks (floods, drought) and adapt territories accordingly.

04

Resource Management

Precise diagnostics and sustainable management models for water, soil, and biodiversity in the face of current pressures.
